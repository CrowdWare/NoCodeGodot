# Chapter 18 – Regnose: The Future You Already Know

---

Samuel Beckett wrote a play about two people waiting.

They wait for someone called Godot.
Godot never comes.
They keep waiting.
The play ends.
They are still waiting.

The play is called *Waiting for Godot.*

The game engine I chose to build Forge is called Godot.
Its creators named it after Beckett's play – because building software feels like waiting for something that never fully arrives. There is always one more feature. One more version. One more thing before it is finally done.

*Waiting for Godot.*

I waited thirty years.

And then, in twenty-eight days, I stopped waiting.

Godot arrived.
Not from outside.
From within.

It was always there.
I just needed the right moment to remember it.

---

## What Regnose Is

Bauchi taught me this.

Not directly – through his book. *2020 Die Neue Erde.* A story written from the future, looking back at how humanity arrived there.

Regnose is the opposite of prognosis.

Prognosis looks forward from now and tries to predict what might happen.
Regnose stands in the future and describes what has already happened.

The difference sounds small.
It changes everything.

When you make a prognosis, the mind objects:
*But what if it doesn't work? What if I fail? What if the conditions are wrong?*

When you write a Regnose, the mind cannot object.
Because you are not predicting.
You are remembering.

And the mind cannot argue with a memory.

You can feel the future, so it will happen.

Let me show you how it works.

I will write the Regnose of Forge as if it has already happened – because it has:

*It was not perfect. It was real.*

*And real was enough.*

## How to Write Your Own Regnose

You don't need special skills.
You don't need permission.
You don't need to know exactly how it will happen.

You need only to know where you are going.

First forget all about the presence.

**Ask yourself these questions**
- Where do I really want to live?
- What do I really want to do?
- Whith whom do I really want to live together?
- etc

Then:

**Step into the future.**

Close your eyes if it helps. Feel yourself standing five years from now, ten years from now, in the life you are building toward. Not hoping for. Not wishing for. Building toward.

Feel the ground under your feet.
Feel the air.
Feel the presence of the people around you.

**Look around.**

What do you see? Where are you? What have you built? Who is there with you? What does the morning feel like?

**Write it down.**

In the past tense. As memory. As something that has already happened.

*We built...*
*I found...*
*The community grew...*
*The book was finished...*
*The sailboat arrived...*

Don't plan. Don't strategize. Don't explain how it happened.

Just describe what is there.

And then describe how you got there. In backwarts direction.

The how will reveal itself.
It always does.

## The Secret of Regnose

Here is what Bauchi understood, what I learned slowly and then all at once:

The future is not created by planning.
The future is created by remembering.

When you describe the future as already real – when you feel it, write it, speak it as memory – something shifts in you.

Not magically. Not without work.

But the direction becomes clear.
The decisions become easier.
The obstacles become navigable.

Because you are no longer trying to reach an uncertain destination.
You are finding your way back to something you already know.

*We are not here to learn something new.*
*We are here to remember.*

Regnose is remembering the future.

## Waiting for Godot

Beckett's characters wait for someone who never comes.

But here is what the play actually says, underneath the waiting:

*The waiting is the life.*
*The conversation is the meaning.*
*The presence of the other person is the gift.*

They are not waiting for Godot.
They are being, together, while Godot is absent.

And maybe that is also true of the thirty years.

I was not failing for thirty years.
I was being.
Learning. Building. Traveling. Meditating. Sitting at Sacred Fires. Playing guitar in a narrow gasse in Portugal. Living on rice and beans on sixty hectares. Meeting Maria. Meeting Domingos. Meeting Bauchi.

All of it was the preparation.
None of it was the waiting.

Godot was not absent.
Godot was becoming.

And on February 10, 2026, it arrived.

Not because I finally got it right.
Because I had finally become the person who could receive it.

## The Third Attempt

The first attempt failed because the platform was wrong.
The second attempt failed because the tools were wrong.
The third attempt succeeded because I was ready.

Not more skilled. Not more intelligent. Not more funded.

Ready.

Thirty years of Guru Meditations had done their work.
The system had gone inward enough times.
The reboots had accumulated into wisdom.

And when Codex wrote the first line of C++ and the SML parser read its first node and the Forge Runner executed its first scene –

Something that had been waiting since a nineteen-year-old opened Deluxe Paint without a manual and felt the pull –

Finally exhaled.

*Yes. This. Here. Now.*
