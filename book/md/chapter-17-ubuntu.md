# Chapter 17 – Ubuntu: I Am Because We Are

---

Let me ask you something.

Imagine you were not born in Germany. Not in the US. Not in any country at all.

You were born on an island. A beautiful island. With 40 other people. Your tribe.

No government. No tax office. No job center. No LinkedIn profile.

Just the island. And the people. And the sun.

---

## A Day in Paradise

You wake up because the sunlight tickles your face through the leaves.

You walk into the garden and pick an apple – because you feel hungry, or because the apple is smiling at you. Both are valid reasons.

You walk to the beach. The waves are perfect today. You grab your kite and spend the morning on the water.

In the afternoon you sit under a tree and think about something that has been on your mind. Maybe you sketch something. Maybe you build something. Maybe you just watch the clouds.

In the evening you come back to the camp. The fire is already burning. Someone has cooked. You grab the guitar and start playing. People gather. Someone starts singing. A child falls asleep in their mother's arms.

---

Now I ask you:

**How many hours did you work today?**

**How much money did you spend?**

---

Take a moment. Really answer it.

Zero. And zero.

And yet: you were fed. You were sheltered. You were surrounded by people who cared. You created something. You played. You rested. You were, completely and fully, alive.

---

## The Constructed Cage

Now someone arrives on your island.

They wear a suit. They carry a clipboard.

They tell you: *"This is inefficient. You need to work. You need money. You need a job. You need to be productive. You need to justify your existence with output."*

And slowly – generation by generation – the island disappears.

Not because it was wrong.
But because someone built a cage around it and called the cage civilization.

The cage is golden. It is comfortable. It has Netflix and delivery food and a career ladder.

And most people never question it because they were born inside it.

George Orwell described a cage built from fear and surveillance.

Our cage is subtler. It is built from the belief that without money, without work, without productivity – you have no right to exist.

**That belief is the cage.**

---

## What Ubuntu Says

Ubuntu is a Nguni Bantu word.

It means: *"I am because we are."*

Not: *"I am because I produce."*
Not: *"I am because I earn."*
Not: *"I am because I am useful."*

Simply: *I am. Because we are.*

Your existence is justified by the fact that you exist. Within a community that exists together.

Michael Tellinger has spent years showing that this is not a romantic fantasy. It is how humans lived for most of their history. The island is not a thought experiment. It is the memory of what we were before the clipboard arrived.

---

## The Oasis Strategy

I am not naive.

I am not saying: destroy the system. Burn the cage. Revolution now.

I have lived outside the system. Van. Libraries. Park benches. Rainbow Gatherings. I know what it costs. I know what it gives.

What I am saying is simpler:

**Build oases.**

Not for everyone. Not by force. Just – build them. Open the door. Let people come if they want.

This is what CrowdWare is for.

Forge generates revenue through commercial licenses. People and companies who want to use Forge in closed products pay for that right. That money flows into a Swiss holding company – structured as a DAO, governed by code, not by people who can be corrupted or replaced.

The DAO buys land.

On that land: communities. Where people can live without rent. Without the cage. Where the apple in the garden is enough. Where the fire in the evening is enough. Where the guitar is enough.

Not forever if they don't want it. Not mandatory. Not ideological.

Just: available. The door is open.

---

## The Infrastructure of Freedom

Here is the practical part.

For most of human history, living outside the system meant living without infrastructure. No electricity. No communication. No connection to the people and tools you love.

That is no longer true.

A 400W solar panel and a battery bank cost less than a month's rent in Berlin.
Starlink costs 40 euros a month and works in the middle of the ocean. In the jungle. On a mountain.

The sailboat that carries me and some brothers and sisters from the Rainbow Family to Costa Rica will have both.

I will write code on that boat. Or I won't. Both are fine.

The point is: **the choice exists now.** Technology has finally caught up with the vision.

You can live on an island – a real one, or a metaphorical one – and still be connected to everything that matters. Still contribute. Still create. Still be part of something larger.

The cage is optional. For the first time in history, it is genuinely optional.

---

## The Invitation

I am building tools so that people can write and publish their own books.

Not because the world needs more books.

Because the world needs more voices.

Your voice. Your vision. Your island.

The system wants you to consume. To scroll. To watch. To buy.

I want you to create. To describe. To manifest.

Orwell's 1984 showed a world where language was used to imprison thought.
This book – and the tools I build – exist to do the opposite.

**Write your vision.**
**Describe your island.**
**Tell the world what you are building.**

Because when enough people describe the same future – it becomes inevitable.

That is Regnose.
That is Ubuntu.
That is the point of all of this.

---

*"I am because we are."*

*And we are because enough of us dared to describe what we already knew was possible.*

---
