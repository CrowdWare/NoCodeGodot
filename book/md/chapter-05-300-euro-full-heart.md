# Chapter 5 – 300 Euro and a Full Heart

---

The mind says: you can't.
The heart says: of course you can.
One of them is always right.
It is never the mind.

---

## The Sacred Fire

It was evening in Potsdam.

A circle of people sat around a fire at a lake. Rainbow Gathering style – no phones, no distraction, just fire and people and the particular quality of attention that only arrives when screens are absent.

Joachim was there.

Joachim, who had traveled from Argentina, who carried stories like other people carry luggage, who somehow always ended up exactly where something important was about to happen.

He asked permission to use his phone at the Sacred Fire.

This was not a small request. At a Sacred Fire, phones are not welcome. The fire is holy. The circle is protected. Technology breaks the spell.

But he asked. And we gave permission.

He read us "The Egg" by Andy Weir.

The whole thing. In English. By firelight. At a lake in Potsdam.

And when he finished –

I laughed.

Not politely. Not briefly.

For fifteen minutes I laughed the laugh of someone who has just been told something they already knew. The laugh that arrives when the universe finally says out loud what it has been whispering for years.

*Of course. Obviously. How did I not see this sooner?*

We are not separate souls moving through separate lives.
We are one soul moving through all lives simultaneously.
Learning. Remembering. Returning.

*Of course.*

Two weeks later I packed my van and drove to Portugal.

---

## 300 Euro

Let me tell you about the financial situation.

Hartz IV – the German social welfare payment – had been sanctioned. Twice. Which meant I was receiving a reduced amount. Which meant I had approximately 300 Euro to my name.

My mind was very clear about what this meant:

*You cannot drive to Portugal on 300 Euro. You don't have enough money for diesel. You won't be able to eat. This is not possible. Stay where you are.*

My heart was equally clear:

*Of course you can. Go.*

I have learned, over many years and many impossible situations, that the mind is a excellent tool for certain things. Optimization. Planning. SQL queries with fifteen tables.

But the mind is not qualified to decide what is possible.

Only the heart knows that.

I drove to Portugal.

---

## Two Months on the Road

The journey took two months.

This surprises people. Portugal is not that far. But I was not in a hurry, and the road had its own agenda.

I stopped in Bern. Found the university, parked at the "Gaswerk", and worked. The Swiss university did not know it was hosting a German developer living in a van on 300 Euro. It didn't need to know. The WiFi worked. That was enough.

I stopped in other places whose names I have half forgotten. Each one taught me something. Each one was exactly where I needed to be.

The mind would have driven straight through.
The heart knew there were stops to make.

At one place I just had to wait for the next money from job center and was able to buy Diesel then. I also me nice guy who read my first book and just wanted to get to know me. And because of the fact that he loved my book he gave me shelter, food and a small job picking apples. Have I mentioned that I love apples. You will find out later in this book, what I mean.

---

## Madrid

In Madrid, someone stole my phone.

Just like that. One moment it was there. The next it wasn't.

With the phone went my navigation. My maps. My ability to know where I was going.

I was left with paper maps and the old skills – reading roads, following signs, making decisions at intersections without a voice telling me which way to turn.

I got lost.

Not a little lost. Two hundred kilometers lost. A detour that added hours to a journey that was already taking months.

My mind was furious.

*This is a disaster. You've wasted time. You've wasted diesel you can't afford. This is exactly what happens when you don't plan properly.*

But here is what I know now:

There are no wrong turns on the right journey.

The two hundred kilometer detour was not a mistake.
It was a route.
To somewhere specific.
For a reason I didn't know yet.

---

## Caldas da Rainha

I couldn't make it to Lisbon.

Not enough diesel. The math was simple and unforgiving.

So I looked at the map and found the closest point of the Atlantic coast. A town I had never heard of. A name that translates as *the hot springs of the queen.*

Caldas da Rainha.

I drove there instead.

The town revealed itself slowly, the way magical places always do. And then I saw them – in the shop windows, on the shelves, in the displays – ceramic penises. Everywhere. Proudly. Joyfully.

The town venerates the Lingam.

I laughed for the second time on this journey.

*Of course it does. Of course this is where I ended up.*

That first night I met two Rainbow brothers.

They taught me to busk.

I already knew how to play guitar – I had played since childhood – but I had never played on the street. Never opened a case and let strangers decide what my music was worth.

They showed me the right gasse. The narrow street that led to the fresh market, where people walked every morning to buy fruit and vegetables. The acoustics were good. The foot traffic was steady. The energy was right.

I played.

People listened. Some dropped coins. One woman dropped fruits. Some smiled. Some stopped for a moment and then continued with their day, carrying a few bars of music with them into the morning.

It was enough.

It was more than enough.

---

## 1984

Across from where I played, there was a bookshop.

One day I went in, looking for a book for a friend. They didn't have it.

The bookseller looked at me – this van-dwelling, guitar-playing, 300-Euro German – and put a book in my hands instead.

*1984* by George Orwell.

I knew the story. I had seen it as a film. But I did not took the book.

Two weeks later, the pandemic began.

I have thought about this many times since.

The bookseller who handed me Orwell's vision of surveillance and control, two weeks before the world restructured itself around surveillance and control.

Coincidence? Perhaps.

Or perhaps the universe was giving me context. Showing me what was coming. Putting the framework in my hands before the picture arrived.

Either way – I know the story from that book. And I understood what I was watching.

---

## Obidos

Near Caldas da Rainha lies a town called Obidos.

I knew this town. I had written about it.

In an earlier book – a book about how to step outside the system, about manifestation and alternative living – I had described Obidos as a place where land was available. Affordable land. Five thousand square meters with fruit trees and nut bushes. Fifteen thousand Euro.

A place where community could begin.

I called the number.

*I'm sorry,* they said. *That land was sold last weekend.*

I asked my Rainbow brothers if they knew who had bought it.

They said: *Probably Maria. She also wants to start a community.*

---

## Maria

I found Maria in Obidos.

She was working there. Portuguese, warm, clear-eyed, the kind of person who has already decided what matters and is simply getting on with it.

I asked her directly.

*Did you buy land?*

*Yes.*

*Do you want to create a community?*

*Yes.*

The next day I moved in.

Two sentences. One decision. A new chapter.

This is how the important things happen. Not through planning. Not through negotiation. Through two people recognizing the same vision and saying yes before the mind has time to object.

Joachim arrived shortly after.

The three of us – a German developer, a Portuguese landowner, an Argentinian wanderer – began building something together in the hills above the Atlantic.

It had no name yet.
It didn't need one.
It already existed.

---

## What the Stolen Phone Actually Did

I have thought about the theft in Madrid many times.

Without it, I would have driven straight to Lisbon.
I would have missed the two hundred kilometer detour.
I would have arrived somewhere with enough diesel.
I would never have ended up in Caldas da Rainha.
I would never have met the Rainbow brothers.
I would never have busked for the first time.
I would never have received 1984 from a bookseller who knew something I didn't.
I would never have found my way to Obidos.
I would never have met Maria.

The thief in Madrid gave me everything that came next.

I hope he needed the phone more than I did.

---

## The Heart Knew

300 Euro.
Sanctioned Hartz IV.
A stolen phone.
A wrong turn.
A town full of ceramic penises.
A book about dystopia.
Two sentences with a stranger.

The mind would have stayed in Berlin.
The mind would have waited for better conditions.
The mind would have made a plan.

The heart said: *go.*

And everything that mattered was waiting on the other side of that decision.

It always is.
